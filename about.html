// about

The same systems-thinking, in different rooms.

I'm a Year 12 student at Haberdashers' Girls' School, applying to study Computer Science at Cambridge. My first real experience of code was programming Bee-Bots to draw shapes — working out the angles needed to draw a perfect cog. That meeting point between mathematical reasoning and computational structure is still what I find most interesting about this subject, whether I'm writing Django models, modelling stochastic processes for a maths competition, or just working out how to run a 500-person conference without anything catching fire.

At 13 I completed Harvard's CS50x. Since then I've tried to make sure everything I build is something a real person would actually use — not a tutorial project, but software with users, bugs, and decisions I had to defend.

// competition mathematics

2nd of 980 teams, Jane Street, NYC

[ M3 Challenge NYC team photo — placeholder ]

In February, my team of five had 14 hours to model a real-world problem for SIAM's M3 Challenge. We chose online sports gambling — building regression models and stochastic processes to explore the mathematics behind betting markets, then building our own GUI in Python and MATLAB to present the results.

As the programmer on the team, placing 2nd of 980 registered teams worldwide for the Technical Computing Award was the proof that the part of this I enjoy most — deciding what's actually worth computing, not just computing everything possible — scales beyond a classroom. We were flown to Jane Street's New York headquarters for the final round, and picked up an additional scholarship for finishing in the top 4% overall.

Team: Arav Mathur, Aarav Rajput, Aarav Shah, Sofia Rusche, and me.

// not as separate as they look

Leadership, in three different uniforms

I've found that most of what I do outside the classroom maps back to the same skills I use writing code — just without the syntax.

Secretary General, HabsMUN 2027

Running a 500+ delegate international conference

I lead the planning of a three-day conference for over 500 students from across the globe, having chaired my school's weekly MUN club and been the youngest student selected to chair a 40-delegate room at HabsMUN 2025. I've made AI regulation the topic of this year's Crisis Committee.

→ The bridge: coordinating 500 people across three days isn't that different from coordinating services across a distributed system — both need clear protocols, graceful failure handling, and someone who's thought about the edge cases in advance.

RAF Cadet — Flight Sergeant

Leading a cyber team to 1st place, Royal Air Squadron Trophy

As a Combined Cadet Force cadet since 2023, I led our 14-cadet squad's cyber team to first place defending a simulated RAF Air Cadets portal against a live hacking attempt, alongside first aid, drill and leadership rounds — placing 3rd overall. The squad is preparing for National Finals next.

→ The bridge: the simulation made me think hard about what the same attack would mean against the real Ministry of Defence — which is most of why I find cybersecurity more interesting than abstract.

Gymnastics Coach, UKCC Level 1

Coaching younger athletes — and learning how to explain things

I coach weekly at E.J.B. Gymnastics Club, mentoring younger athletes through a sport I also compete in at Nationals. It's the same skill that makes my educational content work: breaking something physically and mentally difficult into steps that actually land for the person in front of you.

→ The bridge: teaching a ten-year-old a back walkover and teaching 30,000 students a study technique require the same thing — knowing which step they're actually stuck on.

Senior Prefect — Head of Upper School

Responsible for 375 students, Year 9 to 11

As part of the Class of '27 Senior Prefect Team, I'm responsible for 375 students across three year groups, alongside being a House Sports Representative, Trampolining Team member (competed at Nationals), and Lacrosse Senior Team player (also Nationals). I also won the Audley Twiston Davies Cup for excellence in public speaking and the All-Round Sports Achievement award, 1 of 125 students recognised.

→ The bridge: being responsible for hundreds of people's day-to-day experience at school is the closest thing to product ownership I had before I knew what product ownership was.
